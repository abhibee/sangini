Petra



Petra is a famous archaeological site in Jordan's south-western desert. Dating to around 300 B.C., it was the capital of the Nabatean empire. Accessed by a narrow canyon called Al Siq, it contains tombs and temples which is carved into pink sandstone cliffs, from which it earns its nickname, the Rose City.
